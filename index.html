Bet 02 · Customization

Put generation
in the user’s hands

Problem

Interaction content was preset and one-size-fits-all — users could only receive it passively and never got the scenes or storylines they actually wanted. Novelty wore off fast, engagement stayed shallow, and there was little reason to pay.

Insight + Hypothesis

In chat, users can pick a preset action or type their own DIY prompt, and the character generates bespoke content — images, video, live action — in real time. By making controllability the highlight, users no longer passively receive content; they direct every generation, and that sense of control deepens immersion and drives both sustained engagement and first purchase.

Mechanics → Results
Three bets,
validated results

Every bet was validated against instrumentation, not opinion: did it actually lift ROI and top-ups? When the data said no, I switched strategy and kept iterating until the numbers genuinely moved. Here’s what I’d carry forward.

Reflection

I treated every bet as a hypothesis to be proven by data, not shipped on conviction. Each one was instrumented to ask a single question — did it actually lift ROI and top-ups? When the numbers didn’t move, I dropped the idea and tried another angle, iterating until the data showed a real gain. That validate-or-switch loop is what I’d carry into the next product.

My contribution

As the designer on these bets, my job was to turn each problem into a UI/UX solution that moved the numbers. I led the solution for every bet, read the funnels and ran the reviews to decide what to keep or swap, designed the right UI/UX for each fix, and kept iterating until the data showed a genuine lift in conversion, ARPU and top-ups.
